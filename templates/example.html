Example


A patient in her first pregnancy with twins is admitted to the obstetric emergency room due to concerns about preeclampsia. She is currently at 31+4 weeks gestation. She had a previous hospitalization two weeks ago with similar complaints, and no treatment was administered then.

During her first trimester, her cffDNA test was positive, and her blood type is B. She has a history of diabetes mellitus (DM) and is nulliparous. Her mean arterial pressure (MAP) was measured at 85 mmHg, and her PLGF levels were 150 pg/mL.

In her second trimester, her MAP increased to 90 mmHg, SFLT1 levels were 50 pg/mL, the SFLT1/PLGF ratio was 3.0, and her PLGF levels dropped to 160 pg/mL.

Currently, in her third trimester, her MAP is 95 mmHg, the SFLT1/PLGF ratio is 4.0, SFLT1 levels are 60 pg/mL, and PLGF levels are 170 pg/mL.

Given these clinical parameters for a twin pregnancy, the tool will calculate her absolute risk for developing preeclampsia.

Summary of Inputs:

  • First Trimester:
    • cffDNA: 14?
    • B: 1
    • DM: 1
    • Nulliparous: 1
    • MAP: 85 mmHg
    • PLGF: 150 pg/mL
  • Second Trimester:
    • MAP: 90 mmHg
    • SFLT1: 50 pg/mL
    • SFLT1/PLGF Ratio: 3.0
    • PLGF: 160 pg/mL
  • Third Trimester:
    • MAP: 95 mmHg
    • SFLT1/PLGF Ratio: 4.0
    • SFLT1: 60 pg/mL
    • PLGF: 170 pg/mL
